Creating 8 bit Music
Information

This website was made for you to have fun to recreate famous 8 bit music from retro games but also for you to create your own 8 bit music.

As the center of the page, you can see a virtual keyboard.
This keyboard represents your actual keyboard.
Moreover, each touch of the keyboard is mapped to a musical note.
For example, tapping on the Q key will create the B4 note.

Furthermore, thanks to the button in the 1st box on the left of the screen, you can change the waveforms of the oscillator used.
In fact, most retro games used pulse, square, triangle and noise oscillator for their music. This is what I tried to recreate here.

The second box on the left is to control the recording.
You can either start the recording or end it.

The third box on the left is to control the recording you have just made.
You can either play it or stop it.

The fourth box is to add effect to your sound. You can add a chebyshev and a reverb effect.
In addition, you can add a chebyshev/reverb effect to the oscillator that you want thanks to the 1st box on the left.
Clicking on "triangle" waveform, and then by manipulating the slider of reverb effect, you will affect the reverb effect on the triangle oscillator.

The last box on the left is to redirect you to this webpage to give some information on how to use the website.

Under the virtual keyboard, there is a part for the visualization of the sound. Each time, you type on the keyboard, the squares will be filled randomly. The color of the filling corresponds to the "waveform" chosen.

At the bottom of the page, you can click on two buttons (Zelda or Mario) to listen to famous 8 bit games musics.
These two 8 bit musics were recreating using the same principe I'm using in this website.
This is the website where I took them:
https://codepen.io/gregh/post/recreating-legendary-8-bit-games-music-with-web-audio-api
I can also mention that while playing the music you can see the keys being played in the music will light up in the virtual keyboard according to the mapping I have defined between the keys of the keyboard and the musical notes.
By changing the "waveforms" (1st box on the left), you can see the key corresponding respectively to the pulse synth, square and triangle oscillator.
The noise doesn't depend on the musical note played. Thus, randomly, I choose to light up the Q key on the virtual keyboard.
